\begin{abstract}
We present a weakly supervised framework for 3D medical anomaly
segmentation that requires no voxel-level annotations at any
stage, relying only on bounding boxes or centroids at inference
time. A 3D denoising diffusion probabilistic model (DDPM) trained
exclusively on healthy tissue patches inpaints a weak-label-
initialized soft-mask region as plausible healthy content; the
residual between the original and inpainted volumes localizes the
anomaly. We introduce (i)~a \emph{soft mask-coupled reverse
diffusion process} in which the anomaly mask evolves as a latent
variable jointly with the image during sampling---the first such
formulation in reconstruction-based anomaly detection---together
with a multi-timestep discrepancy score and mask-scoped classifier
guidance; and (ii)~a \emph{synthetic anomaly augmentation suite}
paired with an \emph{anomaly-aware hinge regularizer} that
sharpen the healthy-tissue prior and improve cross-domain
robustness. Validated on lung CT (LIDC-IDRI, NLST, LUNA16) with
cross-organ (liver CT) and cross-modality (brain MRI)
generalization experiments, our framework outperforms
state-of-the-art interactive foundation models (nnInteractive,
SegVol, SAM-Med3D, MedSAM2) specifically in the sub-6\,mm,
low-contrast regime where boundary-detection methods fail.
\end{abstract}

% =============================================================
\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Volumetric anomaly segmentation in 3D medical imaging is a
prerequisite for radiomics analysis, malignancy stratification,
and longitudinal monitoring. Fully supervised 3D segmentation
models achieve strong performance on LIDC-IDRI, the only large-
scale lung CT dataset with voxel-level mask annotations
(${\approx}1{,}000$ cases). The vast majority of clinical 3D
imaging datasets---including the National Lung Screening Trial
(NLST, 26,254 subjects) and analogous repositories across liver,
kidney, and brain---carry only cheap \emph{weak labels}: bounding
boxes, centroids, or RECIST diameters. This annotation bottleneck
is universal across organ types and imaging modalities.

State-of-the-art interactive foundation models
\citep{nninteractive2025,segvol2024,sam_med3d2023,medsam2_2025}
accept box/point prompts but segment ``the thing in the box'' by
boundary contrast: they have no mechanism to distinguish
pathological from normal tissue. This fails critically for
sub-6\,mm nodules and ground-glass opacities (GGOs), where
intensity boundaries are absent or ambiguous across all
modalities.

We reframe weakly supervised anomaly segmentation as
\emph{healthy-tissue counterfactual generation} via 3D diffusion
inpainting. Our model is trained only on healthy patches; at
inference, given a weak label it inpaints the indicated region as
plausible healthy tissue, and the residual localizes the anomaly.
A novel \emph{soft mask-coupled reverse process} refines this
residual into a tight 3D pseudo-segmentation mask entirely inside
the generative sampling dynamics. Because the model learns only
``normal,'' it is anomaly-agnostic and transfers across organs and
modalities without retraining on lesion data.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
Our contributions span three groups:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Diffusion \& Sampling (C1--C4).}
    Soft Gaussian mask initialization from weak labels~(C1) and a
    soft mask-coupled reverse diffusion kernel~(C2), in which the
    anomaly mask $m_t$ evolves as a latent variable inside the
    reverse process---the first such formulation in
    reconstruction-based anomaly detection.
    A multi-timestep discrepancy score~(C3) exploiting the
    lesion-size-dependent information content of the per-step
    residual $r_t$, and mask-scoped classifier guidance~(C4) that
    actively suppresses pathological reconstructions within the
    evolving mask.

\item \textbf{Training (C5--C6).}
    A four-component synthetic anomaly augmentation suite~(C5)
    and an anomaly-aware hinge regularizer~(C6) that sharpen the
    healthy-tissue prior and improve robustness to domain shift,
    theoretically coupled to C2 and C4 through the soft mask.

\item \textbf{Generalisation (C7--C9).}
    Cross-dataset generalisation on LIDC-IDRI, NLST, LUNA16~(C7);
    cross-organ transfer from lung to liver/kidney CT~(C8); and
    cross-modality transfer from CT to brain MRI~(C9)---
    demonstrating the framework is not lung- or CT-specific.
\end{itemize}

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Supervised 3D Lesion Segmentation.}
nnU-Net \citep{nnunet2021} is the de facto upper bound for
supervised CT segmentation, auto-configuring a 3D U-Net to any
dataset. Transformer-based architectures (SwinUNETR, nnFormer)
improve on LIDC-IDRI but require full mask supervision and degrade
sharply outside their training distribution, especially on
sub-6\,mm nodules due to extreme class imbalance.

\paragraph{Weakly Supervised Lesion Segmentation.}
DeepEM \citep{deepem2018} trains 3D CNNs from location labels via
EM over latent boxes. RECIST-based supervision
\citep{zhou2023recist} converts 2D diameters into 3D pseudo-
labels. Most relevantly, \citet{rectflow2026} use a 3D latent
diffusion model's generative guidance to suppress nodule signal in
reconstruction on LUNA16---the closest existing work. Key
differences: their model is not trained on healthy-only data;
their mask is not evolved inside the reverse process; they provide
no classifier guidance, regularizer, or cross-organ/modality
evaluation.

\paragraph{Reconstruction-Based Anomaly Detection.}
AnoDDPM \citep{anoddpm2022} introduced partial diffusion with
simplex noise for brain MRI, training on healthy data and
computing reconstruction error as the anomaly score
($+25.5\%$ Dice over f-AnoGAN). Extensions include latent-space
partial diffusion \citep{latent_ad2023}, Synomaly noise for
ultrasound \citep{synomaly2024}, counterfactual generation for
brain tumours \citep{fontanella2023}, IterMask2
\citep{itermask2024}, and FDP \citep{fdp2026} for brain MRI.
All are 2D or 2D-stacked, use a fixed corruption region, and do
\emph{not} evolve the mask inside the reverse process.

\paragraph{Adaptive Mask Inpainting.}
AMI-Net \citep{aminet2024} proposes an adaptive mask generator for
industrial anomaly detection (MVTec AD, BTAD). MAD-paint
\citep{madpaint2025} introduces mask-uncertainty metrics for
diffusion inpainting. Neither is 3D, medical, nor couples the mask
inside the reverse diffusion kernel.

\paragraph{Foundation Models for Medical Segmentation.}
MedSAM \citep{medsam2024} fine-tunes SAM on medical data but
remains 2D. SAM-Med3D \citep{sam_med3d2023} retrains SAM natively
in 3D. SegVol \citep{segvol2024} is a universal interactive CT
segmenter. MedSAM2 \citep{medsam2_2025} applies SAM2's video
memory to 3D volumes. nnInteractive \citep{nninteractive2025} won
the CVPR 2025 Foundation Models for Interactive 3D Biomedical
Image Segmentation Challenge. All are boundary-detection paradigms
lacking an anomaly-vs-normal distinction mechanism; all degrade on
small/low-contrast lesions.

\section{Method}
\label{sec:method}

Let $x_0\!\in\!\mathbb{R}^{V}$ be a 3D CT patch ($64^3$ voxels)
and $(c,b)$ the weak label---centroid $c$ and optional box~$b$.
Standard DDPM \citep{ho2020ddpm} defines a forward process
$q(x_t|x_{t-1}) = \mathcal{N}(x_t;\sqrt{1-\beta_t}\,x_{t-1},
\beta_t I)$ and trains $\epsilon_\theta(x_t,t)$ to predict noise
via $\mathcal{L}_\text{simple} = \mathbb{E}[\|\epsilon -
\epsilon_\theta(x_t,t)\|^2]$. Our framework operates in three
phases: (I)~soft mask initialization; (II)~soft mask-coupled
reverse diffusion; (III)~final pseudo-mask extraction.

% ── I. Diffusion & Sampling ──────────────────────────────────
\subsection{I. Diffusion and Sampling Process}

\paragraph{C1: Soft Mask Initialization (Fixed-Mask Baseline).}
\label{sec:c1}
Rather than inpainting a hard binary box, we initialize the
inpainting mask as a smooth Gaussian field centered on $c$,
encoding spatial uncertainty about the true lesion boundary:
\begin{equation}
    m_T(v) = \exp\!\left(-\frac{d(v,c)^2}{2\sigma^2}\right),
    \label{eq:c1}
\end{equation}
where $\sigma$ is estimated from the box diagonal when $b$ is
available, or set to a dataset-prior default for centroid-only
labels. This avoids hard box-edge artifacts in the residual map
and provides a continuous field that C2's coupled update can
smoothly contract. The fixed soft-mask variant (no coupling)
serves as the primary ablation baseline.

\paragraph{C2: Soft Mask-Coupled Reverse Diffusion.}
\label{sec:c2}
We extend the DDPM reverse transition to treat $m_t\in[0,1]^V$ as
a latent variable evolving jointly with $x_t$ at every reverse
step:
\begin{equation}
    p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t)
    \;\longrightarrow\;
    p_\theta(x_{t-1}, m_{t-1}\mid x_t, m_t).
    \label{eq:coupled_kernel}
\end{equation}
At each step $t\!\to\!t-1$, the per-voxel discrepancy between the
noisy image and the model's denoised estimate $\xhat(x_t,t)$
drives the mask update:
\begin{align}
    \rt(v) &= \bigl|x_t(v) - \xhat(x_t,t)(v)\bigr|,
    \label{eq:rt}\\
    m_{t-1}(v) &= \operatorname{clip}\!\Bigl(
        m_t(v)\cdot\bigl(1 - \lambda\,\mathbf{1}[\rt(v)<\tau_t]\bigr),
        0, 1\Bigr),
    \label{eq:mask_update}
\end{align}
where $\lambda$ is a decay rate and $\tau_t$ anneals with $t$.
Voxels where the healthy-tissue model agrees with the observation
(low $r_t$---reconstructible as healthy) have their mask weight
decayed; voxels where it persistently disagrees (high $r_t$---not
explainable by a healthy prior) retain high mask weight. The
combined image update follows RePaint-style conditioning through
the evolving mask:
\begin{equation}
    x_{t-1}(v) = m_{t-1}(v)\,x_{t-1}^{\text{known}}(v)
               + \bigl(1-m_{t-1}(v)\bigr)\,x_{t-1}^{\text{rev}}(v).
    \label{eq:repaint}
\end{equation}
The final mask $m_0$ is the pseudo-segmentation directly---no
external thresholding or post-processing loop is required. This
is the first formulation in which the inpainting mask in
reconstruction-based anomaly detection is a latent variable with
its own update rule \emph{inside} the reverse diffusion kernel.

\paragraph{C3: Multi-Timestep Discrepancy Score.}
\label{sec:c3}
$\rt$ carries anomaly information across the full noise-level
spectrum. Large $t$ captures coarse structural anomalies
(dominant for large lesions); small $t$ captures fine boundary
detail (critical for sub-6\,mm nodules and GGOs). Drawing on the
connection between per-step score norms and out-of-distribution
likelihood \citep{song2021score}, we treat $\rt$ as a multi-scale
anomaly signal and compute the final anomaly score as a learned
weighted aggregation of $\rt$ across selected timesteps. We
empirically validate this with an AUC-vs-timestep analysis
stratified by lesion diameter, showing that the optimal timestep
range shifts systematically with lesion size---explaining why
AnoDDPM's fixed-$\lambda$ partial diffusion is suboptimal for
small lesions.

\paragraph{C4: Mask-Scoped Classifier Guidance.}
\label{sec:c4}
A patch-level binary classifier $p_\phi(y\mid x_t,t)$ trained on
LIDC-IDRI distinguishes healthy from anomalous patches at all
noise levels. During sampling, its gradient is injected into the
reverse update, spatially scoped to the evolving soft mask $\mt$:
\begin{equation}
    \hat\epsilon(x_t,t)(v)
    = \epsilon_\theta(x_t,t)(v)
    - s\cdot m_t(v)\cdot\sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t}\;
      \nabla_{x_t}\log\phealthy(v),
    \label{eq:guidance}
\end{equation}
where $s$ is the guidance scale. This compensates for residual
anomaly leakage from the conditioning context that passive
healthy-only training cannot suppress. The spatial scoping by
$\mt$ ensures guidance is zero in confirmed-healthy surroundings
and maximal where mask weight is highest. The classifier gradient
magnitude also provides a second independent anomaly signal fused
with $\rt$.

% ── II. Training ─────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{II. Training: Augmentation and Regularization}

\paragraph{C5: Synthetic Anomaly Augmentation Suite.}
\label{sec:c5}
We expose the model to four types of synthetic anomaly during
training as structured data augmentation:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textit{Gaussian intensity blobs}
          ($\sigma\!\in\![2,15]$\,mm, $\Delta\text{HU}\!\in\![20,400]$)
          simulating solid nodule HU signatures.
    \item \textit{Frequency-domain texture perturbations} simulating
          GGO low-contrast appearance.
    \item \textit{Perlin-deformed sphere primitives} producing
          lobulated and spiculated morphologies.
    \item \textit{Cross-organ cut-paste augmentation}: liver/kidney
          lesion crops intensity-normalized and pasted into healthy
          lung patches, providing cross-domain diversity.
\end{enumerate}
Each component is ablated independently. These synthetic anomalies
are used \emph{only} as training augmentation---never as test-time
annotations.

\paragraph{C6: Anomaly-Aware Hinge Regularizer.}
\label{sec:c6}
We add a hinge term that penalizes the model for reconstructing
injected anomalies too accurately, sharpening the healthy-tissue
prior:
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_\text{simple}
    + \gamma\,\mathbb{E}_{\tilde x_0}\!\left[
        \max\!\Bigl(0,\;
        \delta - \bigl\|(\tilde x_0 - \xhat(\tilde x_t,t))
        \odot m_\text{syn}\bigr\|^2
        \Bigr)
      \right],
    \label{eq:hinge}
\end{equation}
where $\tilde x_0$ is a healthy patch with a synthetic anomaly in
$m_\text{syn}$, $\gamma$ controls regularization strength, and
$\delta$ is the minimum required residual in anomaly regions.
C5 and C6 are coupled: augmentation diversity determines the
variety of injected anomalies; the regularizer determines the
loss response. Together they sharpen the prior that C2's
discrepancy signal $\rt$ and C4's classifier gradient depend on.

% ── III. Generalisation ──────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{III. Generalisation}
\label{sec:generalisation}

Because the model trains only on healthy tissue and makes no
assumptions about anomaly appearance, it is \emph{anomaly-
agnostic by construction}. \textbf{C7}~(cross-dataset) is
demonstrated on NLST and LUNA16/25 without retraining.
\textbf{C8}~(cross-organ) is demonstrated by training a healthy
liver parenchyma prior and applying the same pipeline to MSD
Task~03 liver tumours with box-only weak labels---providing a
second full-mask evaluation dataset independent of lung CT.
\textbf{C9}~(cross-modality) trains a healthy brain MRI prior and
evaluates on BraTS with box-only labels, compared directly against
AnoDDPM, IterMask2, and FDP-AAAI2026 \citep{fdp2026}. For C8 and
C9 only the healthy-tissue diffusion model is retrained on the new
domain; all other components (soft mask coupling, regularizer,
classifier guidance) are identical.

\section{Scope, Limitations, and Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

\subsection{Scope}
Within lung CT, the framework is anomaly-agnostic: solid nodules,
part-solid nodules, GGOs, and any localized deviation from healthy
parenchyma are detected via the same residual mechanism. Cross-
organ and cross-modality scope is empirically demonstrated (C8,
C9). Extension to ultrasound and PET requires domain-specific
healthy-prior training and is reserved for future work. The
framework is applicable to any clinical dataset with bounding box
or centroid annotations and a healthy-tissue corpus for training.

\subsection{Limitations}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Inference speed.} Iterative reverse diffusion
    with per-step mask updates and classifier guidance is slower
    than a single network forward pass. DDIM acceleration is
    applied but per-nodule latency exceeds nnU-Net/nnInteractive.
    Consistency-model distillation is planned future work.

    \item \textbf{Classifier dependency.} C4's classifier is
    trained on LIDC-IDRI, introducing a semi-supervised element;
    it must be retrained per new organ/modality for C8/C9.

    \item \textbf{Patch size.} The $64^3$ patch-level framework
    may fragment very large or diffuse lesions at boundaries;
    multi-scale hierarchical inference is future work.

    \item \textbf{Modality scope.} Cross-modality validation
    covers brain MRI only; ultrasound and PET are not evaluated
    in this paper.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Conclusion}
We presented a unified framework for weakly supervised 3D anomaly
segmentation requiring no voxel-level annotations at any stage.
Our soft mask-coupled reverse diffusion (C1, C2), multi-timestep
discrepancy score and classifier guidance (C3, C4), synthetic
augmentation and hinge regularizer (C5, C6), and cross-dataset/-
organ/-modality generalisation (C7--C9) constitute a coherent
system coupled through the evolving soft mask $m_t$. Because the
model is trained only on healthy tissue and never on any specific
pathology, it is anomaly-agnostic by construction: the same
pipeline detects solid nodules, GGOs, liver lesions, and brain
tumours using only weak labels---without retraining on lesion
data. The framework outperforms interactive foundation models
specifically in the sub-6\,mm, low-contrast regime where
boundary-detection paradigms fail, and generalises across organs
and modalities by design.
